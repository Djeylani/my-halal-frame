V. The Shariah Compliance Evaluation Flow

A rigorous, multi-stage evaluation is a necessity. Every idea must pass through this critical filter, which aligns with established Islamic legal methodology, before any development begins. This process ensures ventures are built upon a foundation of Taqwa (piety).

1

Core Prohibition Check

Does the app involve Riba, Gharar, or Maysir? Is the subject matter itself Halal? This is a hard-stop filter. If any aspect is Haram, the idea is discarded.

2

Ethical Imperative Check

Does the app uphold justice, trust, and transparency? Does it avoid causing harm or promoting futility? The spirit of the transaction must be as pure as its letter.

3

Contract Validity Check

Is the transaction based on a valid Islamic contract (e.g., Bay', Ijarah, Ju'alah, Wakalah)? Are all conditions, such as the seller's possession (Qabd), met?

4

Viability & Value Assessment

Only after passing all Shariah checks is the idea assessed for user value, technical feasibility, and a sustainable, Halal operational model.

VI. High-Risk & Non-Viable Mini-App Categories

Breakdown of Problematic Categories

Certain app categories are fundamentally incompatible with Islamic principles. The chart on the right illustrates the primary Shariah concerns associated with these ideas. The presence of Riba, Gharar, and Maysir is dominant in problematic financial tools, often making them impermissible according to scholarly consensus and standards (e.g., AAOIFI).

  • Financial Apps (Trading/Lending): Overwhelmingly problematic due to Riba, Gharar, and Maysir.
  • Gambling & Chance-Based Games: Directly contradict the prohibition of Maysir.
  • Unmoderated Content Platforms: High risk of becoming conduits for Haram content (Quran 24:19), thus cooperating in sin.
  • AI for Religious Rulings: Generating Fatawa via AI is highly dangerous and impermissible, as issuing legal rulings is a responsibility for qualified human scholars only.
